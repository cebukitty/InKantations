Words in Ink

How my morning begins

Sixteen grams of dark roast,
A splash of mist to stay the static,
The burrs spin and the beans
are ground as fine as sand.

Needles go round the coffee grounds,
Then a steady weighted stamp to even it out,
Then comes the pull — a dark and viscous stream,
The crowning achievement a golden crema

Milk and steam creates the velvet foam,
A dusting of cinnamon and sugar,
Whirl with a spoon,
Take a sip, enjoy your morning.

Making Chai

Ingredients:

  • ☐ Cardamom (5 pods) · 3 cloves · 5 peppercorns
  • ☐ 1 tsp fennel · 1 stick cinnamon
  • ☐ Sliced ginger · Ground nutmeg
  • ☐ Ceylon Tea · Milk · Honey

Five green pods to crack and split,
Three cloves for wit,
Five peppercorns for a fiery kick,
Fennel, cinnamon stick
And ginger slice to entice.

Toast them all ‘til the spicy vapors bloom,
And gives a fragrant hug across the room.
Steep in Milk and Ceylon Leaves,
Add honey, strain, then pour. Be at ease.

Poem for a poet

Your verse falls upon my skin,
trailing goosebumps in its wake.
The languid caress of syllables,
the resonance of rhyme—
intoxicating as drops of sweetest wine.

You weave stanzas into tapestries
dyed with ethereal landscapes.
You choose each word
with a jeweler’s eye,
knowing its facets will seduce.

Yet I suspect, beyond the skein
of iridescent words,
lies the throb of a festering wound—
your lyrical seduction
but an anodyne for a jaded soul.
